# Chapter Writing Tips: Hitting 2500+ Characters

## Common Reasons Chapters Fall Short

| Cause | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Dialog-only scene with no narration | Add character internal reactions, body language, environmental details |
| Key moments summarized instead of shown | Expand to full scene: describe time, place, sensory details |
| Transitions glossed over | Show the journey between locations, add encounters along the way |
| Supporting characters feel flat | Give them reactions, questions, emotional responses |
| Technical descriptions too brief | Describe the process, the senses involved, not just the outcome |

## Content Expansion Techniques

### 1. Sensory Layering
Every scene can carry at least 3 of 5 senses: sound, sight, smell, touch, internal state.

### 2. Character Inner Life
Not long inner monologues, but: physical reactions, behavioral tells, contrast between what they say and feel.

### 3. Scene Anchoring
Time of day, weather, season, lighting, location-specific sounds.

### 4. Dialog Rhythm
Pauses, interruptions, physical actions during dialog, character-specific speech patterns.

### 5. Process Description
Describe the steps, not just the result. Show concentration, adjustment, problem-solving.

## The "Runway" Pattern

A chapter typically needs 3-4 substantial scene blocks:

```
Block 1 (600-800 chars): Opening — continuing hook
Block 2 (600-800 chars): Development — conflict, interaction
Block 3 (600-800 chars): Climax — key event, revelation
Block 4 (600-800 chars): Closing — aftermath, next chapter hook
```

## Don't Pad — Expand

"Padding" = irrelevant content. "Expanding" = giving existing content the sensory/emotional/process detail it deserves.
